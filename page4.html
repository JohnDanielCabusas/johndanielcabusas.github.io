ABIOTIC COMPONENT
The biotic elements within savanna grasslands encompass decomposers, producers, and consumers. Producers, such as trees, lichens, mosses, and grasses, utilize photosynthesis to acquire nutrients from sunlight, supporting a diverse array of organisms, including fungi, larger animals, and insects. Consumers, classified as herbivores, omnivores, and carnivores, acquire energy for growth and reproduction by consuming either plants or animals, with herbivores exclusively feeding on plants, omnivores consuming both plants and animals, and carnivores solely preying on animals. Decomposers, including bacteria, fungi, insects, and algae, decompose organic matter to obtain essential nutrients, thereby completing the ecosystem's nutrient cycle.
