TYPES OF SAVANNA

TROPICAL SAVANNA
The tropical savanna biome is marked by tall grasses and occasional trees. While temperatures remain warm to hot throughout the year, rainfall is substantial only during certain months, with dry periods dominating the majority of the year.
SUBTROPICAL SAVANNA
Subtropical savannas, found between the Tropic of Cancer and approximately the 30th parallel in both hemispheres, feature a blend of grasslands and scattered trees. Unlike their tropical counterparts, subtropical savannas have cooler winters and a broader temperature range.
DRY SAVANNA
Dry savanna, also known as dry tropical savanna or thorn woodland, is a type of savanna characterized by a long dry season where rainfall is scarce. Unlike typical savannas that have a distinct wet and dry season, dry savannas experience prolonged periods of drought with very little rainfall throughout the year.
